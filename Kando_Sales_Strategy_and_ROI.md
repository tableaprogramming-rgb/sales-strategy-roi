## 2. Commission Strategy Options

### Option A: Flat Rate (Current Model)
The current model provides a **flat 10% commission on the first year's subscription**.

| Plan | Annual Revenue (50 emp) | Commission (10%) |
|------|:-----------------------:|:-----------------:|
| do Time | ₱174,000 | ₱17,400 |
| do Payroll | ₱141,000 | ₱14,100 |
| Bundle | ₱246,000 | ₱24,600 |

**Pros:** Simple, easy to understand.
**Cons:** No incentive to upsell to the Bundle, no retention incentive, one-time payout.

---

### Option B: Tiered Commission — Reward Volume

Commission rate **increases** as the salesperson closes more accounts per quarter.

| Quarterly Accounts Closed | Commission Rate |
|:-------------------------:|:---------------:|
| 1–3 accounts | 8% |
| 4–6 accounts | 10% |
| 7–10 accounts | 12% |
| 11+ accounts | 15% |

**Example:** A salesperson closing **8 Bundle accounts** (50 employees each) in a quarter:
- First 3 accounts: 8% × ₱246,000 × 3 = **₱59,040**
- Next 3 accounts: 10% × ₱246,000 × 3 = **₱73,800**
- Next 2 accounts: 12% × ₱246,000 × 2 = **₱59,040**
- **Total commission: ₱191,880** for the quarter

**Pros:** Motivates high performers, scales with company growth.
**Cons:** Top earners might plateau; requires tracking infrastructure.

---

### Option C: Product-Weighted Commission — Push the Bundle

Different commission rates per product to **steer sales toward higher-margin plans**.

| Plan | Commission Rate | Commission (50 emp) |
|------|:---------------:|:-------------------:|
| do Time | 7% | ₱12,180 |
| do Payroll | 7% | ₱9,870 |
| **Bundle** | **12%** | **₱29,520** |

**Rationale:** The Bundle at ₱410 is not simply the sum of do Time + do Payroll (₱525), meaning it's already discounted. By offering a higher commission on the Bundle, you drive customers toward the all-in-one solution, increasing stickiness and reducing churn.

**Pros:** Increases average deal size, improves retention (Bundle clients are harder to churn).
**Cons:** May discourage selling individual plans to smaller companies that genuinely only need one module.

---

### Option D: Base + Residual — Long-Term Retention Incentive

Split the commission into an **upfront payment** and a **residual trail** for as long as the client stays.

| Component | Rate | When Paid |
|-----------|:----:|:---------:|
| Upfront (on signing) | 5% of Year 1 | Upon client's first payment |
| Residual (ongoing) | 3% of monthly revenue | Monthly, as long as client is active |

**Example with a Bundle client (50 employees):**
- **Upfront:** 5% × ₱246,000 = **₱12,300**
- **Monthly Residual:** 3% × ₱20,500 = **₱615/month** (₱7,380/year)
- **Year 1 Total:** ₱12,300 + ₱7,380 = **₱19,680**
- **Year 2 Total (residual only):** **₱7,380**
- **Year 3 Total (residual only):** **₱7,380**
- **3-Year Cumulative:** **₱34,440**

**Pros:** Salespeople become invested in client success and retention, building a passive income stream. Reduces churn because the salesperson has a reason to maintain the relationship.
**Cons:** Higher long-term cost to the company. Requires tracking of account status.

---

### Option E: Hybrid — Recommended Model ⭐

Combine tiered volume with product weighting and a small residual.

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Base Commission** | 8% for individual plans, 10% for Bundle |
| **Volume Accelerator** | +2% after 5 accounts/quarter, +4% after 10 accounts/quarter |
| **Retention Bonus** | 2% residual on monthly revenue for accounts retained past 12 months |
| **Upsell Bonus** | One-time 5% bonus when a do Time or do Payroll client upgrades to Bundle |

**Example — Star Performer Quarter (8 Bundle accounts, 50 emp each):**

| Component | Calculation | Amount |
|-----------|-------------|-------:|
| Base (10% × first 5) | 10% × ₱246,000 × 5 | ₱123,000 |
| Accelerated (12% × next 3) | 12% × ₱246,000 × 3 | ₱88,560 |
| **Quarter Total** | | **₱211,560** |

Then starting in Month 13, a **2% residual** kicks in for each retained account:
- 2% × ₱20,500 × 8 accounts = **₱3,280/month** passive income

## 6. Key Metrics to Track

| Metric | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|:------:|----------------|
| **CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)** | < ₱50,000 | Must be recoverable in < 3 months |
| **LTV (Lifetime Value)** | > ₱600,000 | Ensures LTV:CAC ratio > 12:1 |
| **LTV:CAC Ratio** | > 3:1 (target 10:1+) | Investors look for 3:1 minimum |
| **CAC Payback Period** | < 3 months | Fast payback = efficient sales engine |
| **Monthly Churn Rate** | < 3% | Below 5% is healthy for SMB SaaS |
| **Net Revenue Retention** | > 100% | Expansion revenue exceeds churn |
| **Commission-to-Revenue Ratio** | 8–12% | Keeps margins healthy |

## 7. Recommendations

1. **Start with Option C (Product-Weighted)** to establish a sales motion and push Bundle adoption. It's simple enough for early-stage and steers revenue toward high-value plans.

2. **Transition to Option E (Hybrid)** once you have 5+ sales reps and a quarterly cadence. The tiered structure and retention bonus will become powerful motivators at scale.

3. **For Angel Investment**, pitch a ₱2M raise at 10–15% equity. The payback period of ~30 months via profit share is competitive, and the exit multiple potential (12–25x) makes it an attractive deal for early-stage investors in the Philippine market.

4. **Track unit economics obsessively** — CAC, LTV, and churn are the three numbers that will determine whether Kando becomes a ₱200M+ company or stalls at ₱10M ARR.
