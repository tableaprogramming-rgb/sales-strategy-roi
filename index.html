Model A — Flat Rate (10%)

Model B — Tiered Volume
Accts 1–3: ×8%
Accts 4–6: ×10%

Each tier's accounts × that tier rate × AnnualRev, summed.
Model C — Product-Weighted
Bundle: Accounts × AnnualRev × 12%
Others: Accounts × AnnualRev × 7%
Higher rate on Bundle drives upselling behavior.
Model D — Base + Residual
Upfront = AnnualRev × Accounts × 5%
Residual = Price × Emp × RetMonths × Accounts × 3%
Year 1 = Upfront + first 12 months residual. Lifetime includes full retention period.
Model E — Hybrid (Recommended ⭐)
Accts 1–5: ×base (8%/10%)
Accts 6–10: ×(base+2%)
Accts 11+: ×(base+4%)
+ 2% residual after Month 12
Residual = Price × Emp × (RetentionMonths − 12) × 2% × Accounts.
Cost to Company (%)
LifetimeEarnings ÷ TotalLifetimeRevenue × 100
Lower % = more margin retained. Target: 8–12% for sustainable SaaS.

Investor Cumulative Return vs Investment

Monthly Investor Share
MonthlyGrossProfit × Equity%
Gross Profit = Revenue × (1 − Commission% − OpCost%). Investor receives their equity share of this each month.
Cumulative Investor Return
Σ (MonthlyGrossProfit × Equity%) over 36 months
Running sum of monthly shares. Break-even is when this equals the Investment Amount.
Break-Even Month (Profit Share)
First month where CumulativeReturn ≥ InvestmentAmount
This is the payback via operating profit share only. Exit valuation return happens separately at sale.
Cash Runway
InvestmentAmount ÷ MonthlyBurnRate
Months the investment keeps the company running before revenue must cover all costs. E.g. ₱2M ÷ ₱350K = ~5.7 months.
Company Exit Valuation (Year 3)
ARR (Month 36) × ExitMultiple (e.g. 7x)
SaaS companies typically valued at 5–10x ARR. Higher growth = higher multiple.
Investor Exit Value
CompanyValuation × Equity%
What the investor's shares are worth at exit. ROI Multiple = ExitValue ÷ InvestmentAmount.

Key Takeaways

  • Hybrid Commission (Model E) is recommended as it aligns sales incentives with company goals (retention and upsells), while keeping effective commission costs around 10-12%.
  • Client Retention is the biggest lever for long-term growth. Even a 2% improvement in churn dramatically increases Year 3 ARR.
  • The Angel Investor proposal is highly attractive at 12.5% equity, provided the team hits the target of 30 clients in Year 1.
